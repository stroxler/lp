Let’s look at the titles in the names

A quick look at the names shows several different titles in use. A quick look at some of the most common shows nothing very surprising; many of them we could guess from the age and Sex, although the distinctions between “Miss” and “Mrs” and also the use of “Don”/“Donna” might give us some additional information.

(Note that when using stringr::str_detect, we need to use “\.” to escape the periods; the first backslash prevents R itself from interpreting the second backslash, which tells the regexp matcher to treat the period as literal rather than as “any character”.)

We can also create a function (which we will probably add to our package when we begin working on a full analysis) to extract the titles we saw from the names:

names_to_titles <- function(names) {
  titles = rep("Other", length(names))
  titles[str_detect(names, "Mr\\.")] = "Mr."
  titles[str_detect(names, "Mrs\\.")] = "Mrs."
  titles[str_detect(names, "Miss\\.")] = "Miss."
  titles[str_detect(names, "Master")] = "Master"
  titles[str_detect(names, "Don\\.")] = "Don."
  titles[str_detect(names, "Donna\\.")] = "Donna."
  as.factor(titles)
}
names_to_titles(combined$Name[1:10])
#>  [1] Mr.    Mrs.   Miss.  Mrs.   Mr.    Mr.    Mr.    Master Mrs.   Mrs.  
#> Levels: Master Miss. Mr. Mrs.
